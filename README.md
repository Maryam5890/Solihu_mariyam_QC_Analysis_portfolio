This portfolio documents my hands-on work as a Quality Control Data Analyst Intern at Polar Petrochemical Limited. It covers the full process from raw handwritten lab registers to structured datasets, trend charts, and management-ready reports.

The core question driving this project: *"Is product quality getting better, worse, or staying stable?"* Every chart and table here is built to answer that.

## What's Inside

### 📋 QC Parameter Log
Monthly laboratory records for 15 batches across January–July 2024, covering:
- Kinematic Viscosity at 40°C and 100°C
- Viscosity Index (VI)
- Flash Point (°C)
- Water Content (%wt)
- Fill Volume and Volume Tolerance
- QC Status — Pass / Review / Fail

Both **Raw Material Intake** and **Finished Goods** stages are tracked and compared.

---

### 📊 Trend Analysis (4 Charts)

| Chart | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| Viscosity Index — Monthly Average | Whether lubricant quality is improving over time |
| Flash Point — Monthly Average | Compliance with safety and dispatch thresholds |
| Water Content — Incoming Batches | Early warning for moisture contamination risk |
| Batch Pass Rate — All Products | Month-over-month quality performance summary |

---

🔬 Key Findings

- **98.4% overall pass rate** across all batches tracked
- **Average Viscosity Index of 102.3** — within specification for industrial lubricants
- **3 flagged lots** identified for review or failure — all traced to raw material intake stage
- Water content showed a **watch required** trend in mid-year batches, flagged for corrective action
- Finished goods consistently outperformed raw intake in compliance — confirming the production process is adding value
